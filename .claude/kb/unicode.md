# Unicode

## What it is

- Universal character set and encoding standard, maintained by the Unicode Consortium
- The Unicode Consortium is a US-based non-profit consortium of technology companies (Apple, Google, Microsoft, and others) — not a formal intergovernmental body

## History

- Unicode project started in 1987 by engineers at Xerox and Apple
- Unicode Consortium incorporated in 1991
- Unicode 1.0 published in 1991

### Relation with ISO

- ISO 10646 (Universal Character Set) is the ISO counterpart to Unicode — maintained in sync with the Unicode Standard, same character repertoire
- ISO 10646 exists to give Unicode formal international/governmental standing, since government contracts and regulations often require ISO standards rather than consortium standards
- ANSI, JIS, and other national standards bodies have adopted ISO 10646 as their national standard (e.g. JIS X 0221)
