# ANSI (American National Standards Institute)

## What it is

- USA's national standards body; international equivalents include ISO (international), JIS (Japan), DIN (Germany)
- A private, non-profit organisation that coordinates the development of voluntary standards in the USA and represents the USA in ISO

## History

- Founded in 1918 as the American Standards Association (ASA)
- Renamed USASI (United States of America Standards Institute) in 1966
- Renamed ANSI in 1969

## Character Sets and Encodings

### ASCII (ANSI X3.4)

- The primary ANSI character set standard; defines 128 characters in 7 bits
- Covers the Latin alphabet, digits, basic punctuation, and control characters
- First published in 1963 (as ASA X3.4, before the ANSI rename)
- Foundational to all subsequent character set standards; ISO 646 is the international equivalent, with minor country-specific variations

### Relation with Unicode and ISO

- Unicode's first 128 code points are identical to ASCII — Unicode is a direct superset
- ANSI has adopted ISO 10646 as a national standard, aligning with Unicode
